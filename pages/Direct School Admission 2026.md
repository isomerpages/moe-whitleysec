<h2><strong>2026 Direct School Admission (DSA)</strong></h2>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Direct School Admission for Secondary Schools (DSA-Sec) is a MOE initiative
that provides a pathway for students to gain admission to secondary school
based on their interests, aptitude and potential.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Whitley Secondary School is conducting the DSA exercise to admit Secondary
One students for the academic year&nbsp;2027 under the following talent
areas:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Public Speaking &amp; Debate</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Student Leadership</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Information about the application process, and each talent area (including
the selection criteria), is provided in the sections below.</p>
</li>
</ol>

<h3><strong><em>(1) Public Speaking and Debate @ Whitley Secondary School</em></strong></h3>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>In today’s world, the ability to speak clearly and think critically matters.
At Whitley Secondary School, our 4-year Communicative Arts Programme gives
you a clear pathway to grow as a confident communicator.</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/WhatsApp_Image_2026_04_23_at_2_19_57_PM.jpg">
</div>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Secondary 1</strong>: Build confidence through dramatic storytelling
and performance, guided by theatre professionals, and experience live theatre.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Secondary 2</strong>: Develop persuasive communication through
advocacy, media production, and meaningful speaking opportunities at school
events.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Secondary 3</strong>: Deepen critical thinking through debate,
respond to impromptu questions with confidence, and take on roles such
as emceeing. Compete in public speaking competitions.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Secondary 4:</strong> Analyse media critically, evaluate misinformation,
dialogue with playwrights and directors, and deliver a broadcast on current
affairs.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>From presenting to your class and school, to representing Whitley in competitions,
you will think on your feet and speak with clarity. Along the way, you
build the skills to succeed: our Whitleyans grow into confident performers
and national achievers.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Join us to unlock your potential as an effective communicator.</strong> With
exciting platforms in and beyond school, you will grow in confidence, find
your voice, and make an impact.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<h4><strong><em>Who should apply?</em></strong></h4>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>If you demonstrate confidence in speaking, have a passion for communication,
and are keen to develop your skills holistically, this programme is for
you!</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The following are our criteria for selection:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Very good or excellent conduct grade</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Demonstrates strong public speaking skills (e.g., speeches to audience,
emceeing) and/or strong communication skills (e.g., debates, podcasts,
journalism).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Does well at the selection interview, including:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>delivering a 2-minute prepared speech on a social issue or story</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>responding to questions with confidence</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Competition or oratorical experience, especially at the zonal, national
or international level, is an advantage.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>Applicants who do not have prior experience in public speaking and debating may also apply. The school will assess applicants based on the selection criteria, which may include assessing the potential of the applicants.</em>
</p>
</li>
</ol>

<p><strong><em>Growth Model for Student Leaders</em></strong>
</p>
<p></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students who assume formal leadership roles will undergo and participate
in intentionally designed learning experiences to equip and challenge them
as they grow as a leader. Some instances include workshops to hone new
skills (such as facilitation techniques), leadership camps and/or dialogue
sessions.</p>
<p></p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students will learn about the 5 practices of exemplary leaders and will
be encouraged to apply these practices in their leadership journey. Throughout
their leadership journey, regular review sessions will be held to provide
students with the opportunity to reflect on their leadership experiences,
assess their progress, manage their responsibilities, and celebrate their
achievements. Not only will these experiences shape the attitudes, beliefs
and actions of the student leaders, it also provides them with opportunities
to develop their social-emotional competencies as they grow in self-awareness
and learn how to manage relationships. They will also grow in their emerging
21 century competencies such as critical, adaptive and inventive thinking.</p>
